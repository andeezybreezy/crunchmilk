How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

The calculator adds up every category of vehicle expense and divides by miles driven to find your true per-mile cost.

The basic rule:

  • Fuel cost per mile = fuel price ÷ MPG
  • Depreciation per mile = (purchase price − resale value) ÷ (years × annual miles)
  • Fixed costs per mile = (insurance + maintenance + registration) ÷ annual miles
  • Total cost per mile = fuel + depreciation + fixed costs

The IRS standard mileage rate for 2024 is $0.67/mile. Most personal vehicles cost between $0.40 and $0.80 per mile when all expenses are included.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good cost per mile for a car?

The average cost per mile is about $0.50-$0.70 for a typical sedan. Economy cars can be as low as $0.35/mile, while trucks and SUVs often exceed $0.75/mile. The IRS standard mileage rate of $0.67 is a reasonable benchmark.

Should I include depreciation in cost per mile?

Yes. Depreciation is typically the largest single cost of vehicle ownership, especially in the first 5 years. A $35,000 car that's worth $20,000 after 5 years has lost $3,000/year to depreciation alone.

How does mileage affect cost per mile?

Higher annual mileage spreads fixed costs (insurance, registration) over more miles, reducing cost per mile for those categories. However, higher mileage increases maintenance costs and depreciation rate.

What costs are not included here?

This calculator covers the major costs. Additional expenses like parking, tolls, car washes, loan interest, and traffic tickets could add $0.02-$0.10 per mile depending on your situation.
