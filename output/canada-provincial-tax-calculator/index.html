How It Works

This canada provincial income tax calculator uses established formulas to provide accurate results.

The basic rule:

  • Federal tax: 15% to $55,867, 20.5% to $111,733, 26% to $154,906, 29% to $220,000, 33% above
  • Basic personal amount: federal $15,705 (2024)
  • Combined marginal rate = Federal marginal rate + Provincial marginal rate
  • Effective rate = Total tax / Taxable income x 100

Results are estimates. Consult a professional for critical decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the 2024 federal income tax brackets?

The 2024 federal brackets are: 15% on the first $55,867, 20.5% on $55,867-$111,733, 26% on $111,733-$154,906, 29% on $154,906-$220,000, and 33% on income above $220,000.

Which province has the lowest income tax?

Alberta has the lowest provincial rates with a flat 10% on the first $142,292. Provinces like Quebec and Nova Scotia have the highest combined rates, with Quebec's top rate at 25.75%.

What is the basic personal amount?

The 2024 federal basic personal amount is $15,705, meaning the first $15,705 of income is effectively tax-free at the federal level. Each province has its own basic personal amount.

How does the Quebec tax system differ?

Quebec collects its own income tax separately (not through the CRA) and has its own brackets. Quebec residents receive a federal tax abatement of 16.5% and do not pay the federal portion on some credits.
