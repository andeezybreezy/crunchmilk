How It Works

A cubic yard is 3 feet × 3 feet × 3 feet = 27 cubic feet. This calculator converts dimensions in any unit to cubic yards for any common shape.

The basic rule:

  • Rectangle: Length × Width × Depth. Cylinder: π × (diameter/2)² × height
  • Triangle: ½ × base × height × depth. Trapezoid: ½ × (top + bottom) × height × length
  • All measurements are converted to feet first, then the result in cubic feet is divided by 27 for cubic yards

This is a universal volume calculator. Use it for concrete, gravel, mulch, soil, sand, or any bulk material sold by the cubic yard. For material-specific calculations with weight and cost estimates, use our dedicated calculators.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many cubic feet are in a cubic yard?

There are exactly 27 cubic feet in one cubic yard. A cubic yard is 3 feet long, 3 feet wide, and 3 feet tall (3 x 3 x 3 = 27). To convert cubic feet to cubic yards, divide by 27.

How do I calculate cubic yards from inches?

Convert all measurements to feet first (divide inches by 12), then multiply length x width x depth to get cubic feet, then divide by 27 for cubic yards. Or use our calculator above — it handles unit conversion automatically.

How much area does a cubic yard cover?

One cubic yard covers: 324 sq ft at 1 inch deep, 162 sq ft at 2 inches, 108 sq ft at 3 inches, 81 sq ft at 4 inches, or 54 sq ft at 6 inches deep. The depth determines how far a cubic yard spreads.

How many wheelbarrows are in a cubic yard?

A standard 3-cubic-foot wheelbarrow holds about 1/9 of a cubic yard, so 9 wheelbarrow loads equal one cubic yard. A large 6-cubic-foot wheelbarrow takes about 5 loads. This is useful for estimating labor when moving material by hand.

What weighs more — a yard of gravel or a yard of mulch?

Gravel is much heavier. One cubic yard of gravel weighs 2,500-3,000 lbs. One cubic yard of mulch weighs 400-800 lbs. Concrete weighs about 4,000 lbs per yard, soil about 2,000-2,200 lbs, and sand about 2,700 lbs.

How many cubic yards fit in a pickup truck?

A full-size pickup truck bed holds about 2 cubic yards of mulch, 1 cubic yard of soil, or 0.5-1 cubic yard of gravel or stone (limited by weight, not volume). Never exceed your truck's payload rating — check the sticker on the driver's door jamb.
