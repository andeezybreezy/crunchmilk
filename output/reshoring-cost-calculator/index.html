How It Works

This calculator compares the true total cost of offshore manufacturing against domestic production by accounting for all costs — not just the unit price on the purchase order.

The basic rule:

  • Offshore unit costs are only 20-40% of the true total cost — shipping, tariffs, quality, and overhead add significantly
  • Hidden offshore costs include: shipping (8-15%), tariffs (0-60%), quality defects (3-8%), inventory carrying (5-12%), IP risk (1-5%), and travel/oversight (2-5%)
  • Domestic benefits include: shorter lead times, lower inventory needs, easier quality control, no tariff exposure, and reduced IP theft risk
  • Break-even tariff rate shows at what tariff level reshoring becomes cost-neutral

The Reshoring Initiative estimates that 20-30% of currently offshored manufacturing would be cost-competitive domestically when all hidden costs are properly accounted for. Recent tariff increases have pushed this figure even higher.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the hidden costs of offshore manufacturing?

The biggest hidden costs include: freight and logistics (8-15% of product cost), tariffs and duties (variable), quality defect rates and rework (3-8%), excess inventory from long lead times (5-12% carrying cost), intellectual property theft risk (1-5% estimated), management travel and oversight (2-5%), and supply chain disruption risk. The Reshoring Initiative estimates total hidden costs add 20-40% to the quoted offshore price.

Is reshoring actually happening?

Yes, significantly. The Reshoring Initiative reports that reshoring and foreign direct investment announcements brought back over 350,000 US manufacturing jobs in 2023 alone. Major drivers include tariff uncertainty, supply chain disruptions experienced during COVID, rising overseas labor costs, automation making US production more competitive, and government incentives like the CHIPS Act and IRA.

How do tariffs affect the reshoring calculation?

Tariffs directly increase the landed cost of imported goods. A 25% tariff on a $10 offshore unit cost adds $2.50, bringing it to $12.50 before other costs. For many product categories, tariff rates above 15-30% make domestic production cost-competitive. The recent tariff increases on Chinese goods (up to 60% on some categories) have dramatically shifted the calculation.

What about nearshoring to Mexico instead?

Mexico offers a middle ground: lower labor costs than the US but higher than China, minimal shipping costs and lead times, USMCA trade agreement benefits, similar time zones, and easier quality oversight. For many companies, Mexico is the pragmatic first step before full US reshoring. The calculator can model this by using Mexico as the source country.
