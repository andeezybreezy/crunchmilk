How It Works

Converting oven recipes to air fryer settings is simple. Air fryers use rapid convection heating in a compact space, so they cook faster and at lower temperatures than conventional ovens.

The basic rule:

  • Reduce temperature by 25°F (about 15°C)
  • Reduce cooking time by 20%
  • Check food a few minutes early — every air fryer runs slightly different

This converter applies these rules automatically and includes food-specific adjustments for common items. It works with all air fryer brands including Ninja, Cosori, Instant Pot, Philips, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert oven temperature to air fryer?

Reduce the oven temperature by 25°F (about 15°C) for your air fryer. For example, if a recipe calls for 400°F in the oven, set your air fryer to 375°F. This works because air fryers circulate hot air more efficiently in their smaller cooking chamber.

How do I convert oven time to air fryer time?

Reduce the oven cooking time by about 20%. For example, if a recipe calls for 30 minutes in the oven, cook for about 24 minutes in the air fryer. It's always a good idea to check your food a few minutes before the timer goes off.

Why does an air fryer cook faster than an oven?

Air fryers use rapid air circulation (convection) in a compact space, which heats food more efficiently than a traditional oven. The smaller cooking chamber means hot air reaches the food faster and more evenly, producing crispier results in less time.

Do I need to preheat my air fryer?

Most air fryers benefit from 2-3 minutes of preheating. This helps achieve crispier results, especially for foods like chicken wings, fries, and anything breaded. However, preheating is optional for most recipes and some models preheat automatically.

Does this converter work with all air fryer brands?

Yes. The oven-to-air-fryer conversion rule (reduce temp by 25°F, reduce time by 20%) is universal and works with all brands including Ninja, Cosori, Instant Pot, Philips, COSORI, Chefman, Gourmia, and more. Individual results may vary slightly by model.

Can I convert frozen food instructions for the air fryer?

Yes, but frozen foods need different adjustments. Start with the package instructions as your baseline, reduce temperature by 25°F, but keep the time the same or add 2-3 minutes. Frozen foods need extra time because the air fryer must thaw and cook simultaneously.
