How It Works

This audio sample rate calculator uses established formulas to provide accurate results.

The basic rule:

  • File Size = Sample Rate x Bit Depth x Channels x Duration / 8
  • Max Frequency = Sample Rate / 2 (Nyquist theorem)

Frequently Asked Questions

What sample rate should I record at?

44.1 kHz is sufficient for most music (captures up to 22 kHz, beyond human hearing). Record at 48 kHz for video projects, or 96 kHz if you plan heavy pitch-shifting or time-stretching in post-production.

Does higher bit depth mean better sound?

Higher bit depth means more dynamic range (quieter noise floor), not necessarily better sound. 16-bit gives 96 dB range (fine for playback). 24-bit gives 144 dB range — essential for recording to capture quiet details without noise.
