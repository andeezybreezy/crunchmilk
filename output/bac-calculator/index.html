How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

This calculator uses the Widmark formula, the standard scientific method for estimating blood alcohol content (BAC).

The basic rule:

  • The Widmark formula: BAC = (Alcohol consumed in grams / (Body weight in grams × r)) × 100 − (0.015 × hours)
  • r = Widmark factor: 0.68 for men, 0.55 for women (ratio of body water)
  • One standard drink contains approximately 14 grams of pure alcohol
  • The body metabolizes alcohol at approximately 0.015% BAC per hour

This is an estimate only and should not be used to determine if you are safe to drive. Individual BAC varies based on food intake, medications, tolerance, metabolism, and other factors. When in doubt, do not drive.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the legal BAC limit for driving?

In all 50 US states, the legal limit is 0.08% BAC. For commercial drivers, the limit is 0.04%. Many countries have lower limits. Note that impairment begins well below the legal limit — even 0.02% affects judgment.

What is a standard drink?

In the US, one standard drink contains about 14 grams (0.6 oz) of pure alcohol. This equals approximately: 12 oz of regular beer (5% ABV), 5 oz of wine (12% ABV), or 1.5 oz of distilled spirits (40% ABV/80 proof).

How long does it take to sober up?

The body metabolizes alcohol at roughly 0.015% BAC per hour. After reaching 0.08% BAC, it takes about 5.5 hours to reach 0.00%. Nothing speeds up this process — not coffee, cold showers, or exercise.

Why does body weight affect BAC?

Alcohol distributes through body water. Larger people have more body water, so the same amount of alcohol is more diluted, resulting in a lower BAC. This is why the same number of drinks affects a 120-lb person more than a 200-lb person.

Why do women generally reach higher BAC than men?

Women typically have a higher body fat percentage and lower body water percentage than men of the same weight. Since alcohol distributes in body water (not fat), the same amount of alcohol is more concentrated in women, leading to a higher BAC.

Does food affect BAC?

Yes. Eating before or while drinking slows alcohol absorption, leading to a lower peak BAC. Food doesn't prevent intoxication, but it reduces the rate at which alcohol enters the bloodstream, spreading the effect over a longer time.
