How It Works

The calculator determines epoxy volume from project dimensions, then converts cubic inches to fluid ounces for easy measuring.

The basic rule:

  • Rectangular: volume = length × width × depth (cubic inches)
  • Circular: volume = π × (diameter/2)² × depth
  • 1 fluid ounce = 1.805 cubic inches
  • Add 5–10% extra for waste, overflow, and mixing loss
  • Most epoxy kits use a 1:1 resin-to-hardener ratio by volume

Pour epoxy in layers no thicker than the manufacturer's recommended maximum (usually 1/4" to 2" per pour depending on the product) to avoid overheating. Deep-pour epoxy allows thicker layers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much epoxy do I need for a river table?

Measure the void area: length × average width × depth of the river channel. A 6-foot table with a 6-inch wide, 1.5-inch deep river needs about 324 cubic inches or roughly 180 fluid ounces (1.4 gallons) before waste allowance.

What is the 1:1 ratio for epoxy?

Most art and table epoxy uses a 1:1 mix ratio by volume — equal parts resin and hardener. Some industrial epoxies use 2:1 or other ratios. Always follow the manufacturer's specified ratio for proper curing.

Why does my epoxy overheat?

Epoxy curing is exothermic. Pouring too thick (over the manufacturer's max) traps heat, causing the resin to yellow, crack, or even smoke. Deep-pour epoxy is formulated for thicker layers (2-4 inches) with slower cure times.

How much extra resin should I buy?

Plan for 10-15% extra to account for mixing cups, stir sticks, drips, and uneven surfaces. For river tables with irregular voids, add 20% since the shape is hard to measure precisely.
