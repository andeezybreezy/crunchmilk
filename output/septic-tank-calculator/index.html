How the Septic Tank Calculator Works

This calculator sizes your septic system using bedroom count, daily water usage, and soil percolation rates.

Drain Field = Daily Flow ÷ Soil Application Rate
  • Tank size: Based on bedroom count minimums per code (1,000-1,500 gal)
  • Percolation rate: Gravel (2 min/in), Sand (5 min/in), Loam (20 min/in), Clay (50 min/in)
  • Drain field: Sized by daily water flow and soil absorption capacity
  • Cost estimate: Includes tank, drain field installation, and permits

Frequently Asked Questions

What size septic tank do I need?

Minimum tank size is based on bedrooms: 1-3 bedrooms need at least 1,000 gallons, 4 bedrooms need 1,250 gallons, and 5-6 bedrooms need 1,500 gallons. Higher daily water usage may require a larger tank.

How does soil type affect septic system design?

Soil type determines percolation rate, which affects drain field size. Sandy soil drains fastest, loam is moderate, and clay is slowest. Slower percolation requires a larger drain field to handle the same volume of water.

How much does a septic system cost?

A typical septic system costs $5,000 to $20,000+ installed. The tank alone runs $1,500-$3,000, and drain field costs $2,000-$10,000 depending on soil conditions and system size.

How big should my drain field be?

Drain field size depends on daily water usage and soil percolation rate. A typical 3-bedroom home needs 600-900 square feet in average loam soil, but clay soil may require twice that area.

How long does a septic system last?

A well-maintained concrete septic tank lasts 20-40 years. Drain fields typically last 15-25 years. Regular pumping every 3-5 years and avoiding overloading the system extends its lifespan significantly.

What is a perc test?

A percolation test measures how fast water drains through your soil. A hole is dug, filled with water, and timed. The result determines whether a conventional septic system is feasible and how large the drain field must be.
