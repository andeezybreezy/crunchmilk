Frequently Asked Questions

What is de-dollarization?

De-dollarization is the global trend of countries reducing reliance on the US dollar for trade and reserves. BRICS nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa) are leading efforts to trade in local currencies.

Will the dollar lose reserve currency status?

Most economists expect the dollar to remain dominant for decades but gradually lose share. The USD's share of global reserves has declined from 72% in 2000 to about 58% in 2024.
