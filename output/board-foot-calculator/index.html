How to Calculate Board Feet

A board foot is the standard unit for measuring and pricing hardwood lumber in the United States. One board foot equals 144 cubic inches of wood — a piece 1 inch thick, 12 inches wide, and 1 foot long.

The formula:

Board Feet = Thickness (in) × Width (in) × Length (ft) ÷ 12

Key points:

  • Thickness and width are measured in inches
  • Length is measured in feet
  • For rough hardwood, use the actual rough thickness (4/4 = 1", 8/4 = 2", etc.)
  • For dimensional lumber (2×4, 2×6, etc.), board feet use the nominal dimensions
  • Multiply by the number of pieces for total board feet

Example: A 8/4 walnut board that is 6 inches wide and 8 feet long:

2" × 6" × 8' ÷ 12 = 8.00 board feet

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a board foot?

A board foot (BF) is a unit of volume used to measure and price lumber, especially hardwood. One board foot equals 144 cubic inches — equivalent to a piece of wood 1 inch thick, 12 inches wide, and 12 inches (1 foot) long. It is the standard pricing unit at hardwood lumber yards across the United States.

How do you calculate board feet?

Use the formula: Thickness (inches) × Width (inches) × Length (feet) ÷ 12 = Board Feet. For example, a board that is 1" thick, 8" wide, and 10' long contains 1 × 8 × 10 ÷ 12 = 6.67 board feet. For multiple pieces of the same size, multiply the result by the quantity.

What does 4/4 lumber mean?

The quarter notation (4/4, 5/4, 6/4, 8/4, etc.) describes rough hardwood lumber thickness in quarter-inch increments. 4/4 ("four quarter") means the wood was sawn to 1 inch thick. 5/4 is 1.25 inches, 6/4 is 1.5 inches, 8/4 is 2 inches, 12/4 is 3 inches, and 16/4 is 4 inches. After surfacing (planing), the board will be slightly thinner — for example, 4/4 lumber surfaces to about 13/16 inch.

How much does a board foot cost?

Prices vary widely by species, grade, and region. Typical ranges: Poplar $3–$6/BF, Red Oak $4–$8/BF, White Oak $5–$10/BF, Hard Maple $5–$10/BF, Cherry $6–$12/BF, Walnut $8–$16/BF, Purpleheart $10–$15/BF, and Ebony $25+/BF. Thicker stock (8/4 and above) typically costs more per board foot than 4/4. FAS (First and Seconds) grade commands the highest prices.

What's the difference between nominal and actual lumber sizes?

Nominal size is the name given to dimensional lumber at the store (e.g., 2×4), while the actual size is smaller due to drying and planing. A 2×4 actually measures 1.5" × 3.5". A 1×6 is actually 0.75" × 5.5". For board foot calculations on dimensional lumber, the nominal dimensions are traditionally used. For rough hardwood, the actual rough thickness (4/4, 8/4, etc.) is used since it hasn't been planed.

How many board feet in a 2×4×8?

A 2×4×8 contains 5.33 board feet. Using the formula with nominal dimensions: 2" × 4" × 8' ÷ 12 = 5.33 BF. Note that lumber yards sell dimensional lumber (2×4, 2×6, etc.) by the linear foot, not by the board foot. Board foot pricing is primarily used for hardwood lumber.
