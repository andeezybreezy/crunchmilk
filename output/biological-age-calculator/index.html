How It Works

This calculator estimates biological age by scoring key health metrics and lifestyle factors against your chronological age. Each factor can add or subtract years, reflecting how these markers correlate with aging in population studies.

The basic rule:

  • Resting heart rate: <60 bpm subtracts years (strong cardiovascular health); >80 adds years
  • Blood pressure: <120 systolic is optimal; 140+ significantly accelerates biological aging
  • Exercise: regular moderate-to-vigorous exercise is the strongest anti-aging factor, subtracting up to 5+ years
  • Sleep: 7-8 hours is optimal; chronic sleep deprivation (<6 hrs) or excess (>9 hrs) adds years

Biological age is a concept — no single calculator can measure it precisely. Clinical biological age tests (epigenetic clocks like Horvath, GrimAge, or TruDiagnostic) analyze DNA methylation patterns and are far more accurate. This calculator uses lifestyle-correlated estimates as a general guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is biological age?

Biological age measures how well your body is functioning relative to your chronological age. Two 50-year-olds can have very different biological ages based on their health, fitness, and lifestyle. Someone with excellent cardiovascular health, regular exercise, and good diet might have a biological age of 40, while a sedentary smoker could have a biological age of 60+.

How accurate is this biological age estimate?

This calculator provides a rough estimate based on lifestyle factors correlated with aging in population studies. For a clinical-grade measurement, consider epigenetic age tests (TruDiagnostic, Elysium Index) that analyze DNA methylation — the gold standard for biological age measurement. Costs range from $200-$500 per test.

What has the biggest impact on biological age?

Research consistently shows exercise is the single most powerful anti-aging intervention. Regular moderate exercise (150+ min/week) can reduce biological age by 5-10 years. Other high-impact factors include not smoking, maintaining a healthy weight, quality sleep (7-8 hours), stress management, and a nutrient-rich diet.

Can you reverse your biological age?

Yes, to a degree. Studies show that lifestyle interventions can measurably reduce biological age. A 2023 study showed participants reduced their epigenetic age by an average of 4.6 years through diet, exercise, sleep optimization, and stress management over 8 weeks. Quitting smoking and starting regular exercise show the largest effects.

How does stress affect biological age?

Chronic stress accelerates aging through multiple pathways: increased cortisol damages telomeres, promotes inflammation, disrupts sleep, and increases cardiovascular risk. Studies show chronically stressed individuals can have biological ages 5-10 years older than their chronological age. Meditation, therapy, and regular exercise are effective countermeasures.

Does diet quality really affect how fast I age?

Significantly. The Mediterranean diet has been shown to slow epigenetic aging by 1-3 years. Diets high in processed foods, added sugar, and refined carbohydrates promote inflammation and oxidative stress, accelerating aging. Key anti-aging foods include fatty fish, leafy greens, berries, nuts, olive oil, and fermented foods.
