How It Works

Planning food for a BBQ party is part math, part strategy. The key is estimating per-person amounts for meat, sides, and drinks, then adjusting for your crowd and party length.

The basic rule:

  • Meat: Plan ⅓ to ½ lb of cooked meat per person. That's about ½ to ¾ lb raw (meat shrinks 25-40% when cooked).
  • Sides: Plan about ½ cup (4 oz) of each side dish per person, with 2-3 side options.
  • Drinks: Plan 2-3 drinks per person for a standard party, more for longer events.
  • Buns & bread: 2 buns per person for burger/dog parties, plus extra for big eaters.

These estimates include a 10-15% buffer so you're not caught short. It's always better to have leftovers than to run out. Adjust up for hungry crowds and long events, down for lighter affairs with many children.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much meat do I need per person for a BBQ?

Plan for ⅓ lb of cooked meat per adult (about ½ lb raw). For a mixed BBQ with burgers, hot dogs, and chicken, figure 2 burger patties OR 2 hot dogs OR 1 chicken breast per person, plus a little extra.

How many burgers per person for a BBQ?

Plan 2 burgers per adult and 1-1.5 for kids. Use ⅓ lb patties for regular burgers or ¼ lb for sliders. Always make 10-15% extra — some people eat 3, and burgers are great as leftovers.

How many hot dogs per person?

Adults typically eat 2 hot dogs, kids eat 1-1.5. If hot dogs are the only protein, bump up to 3 per adult. If you're also serving burgers and chicken, 1-2 per person is enough.

How much pulled pork per person?

Plan ⅓ lb of cooked pulled pork per person for sandwiches. Pork butt loses about 40% of its weight when cooked, so you need about ½ lb raw per person. A 10-lb butt yields about 6 lbs of pulled pork, serving 18 people.

How many drinks per person for a party?

Plan 2-3 drinks per person for a 3-4 hour party. For longer events, add 1 drink per additional hour. Mix of beer, soda, and water. For an all-day BBQ, plan 4-5 drinks per person total.

How much ice do I need for a BBQ?

Plan ½ to 1 lb of ice per person for chilling drinks, plus extra for coolers holding food. A 20-person party needs about 15-20 lbs of ice. In hot weather, increase by 50%.
