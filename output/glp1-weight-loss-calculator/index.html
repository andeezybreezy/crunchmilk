How It Works

This calculator projects weight loss over time using average results from major GLP-1 receptor agonist clinical trials. It models the typical weight loss curve — rapid loss in months 1-6, gradual slowing through month 12, and plateau around months 15-18.

The basic rule:

  • Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4mg): ~15% average body weight loss over 68 weeks (STEP 1 trial)
  • Ozempic (semaglutide 1mg): ~10-12% body weight loss over 68 weeks (SUSTAIN trials, lower dose than Wegovy)
  • Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide 15mg): ~22.5% body weight loss over 72 weeks (SURMOUNT-1 trial)
  • Weight loss follows a logarithmic curve — fastest in early months, plateauing as the body adapts

These projections reflect population averages from clinical trials. About one-third of patients exceed the average, one-third hit the average, and one-third fall below. Diet, exercise, dose titration, and genetics all affect individual results. Discuss expectations with your prescriber.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can I lose on Ozempic vs Wegovy?

Wegovy typically produces more weight loss than Ozempic because the maintenance dose is higher (2.4mg vs 1mg semaglutide). Clinical trials show about 15% body weight loss with Wegovy vs 10-12% with Ozempic over 68 weeks. Both are semaglutide — Wegovy is specifically FDA-approved for weight management at the higher dose.

Is Mounjaro or Zepbound more effective than Wegovy?

Clinical trial data suggests tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) produces greater weight loss than semaglutide (Wegovy) — about 22.5% vs 15% body weight loss. The SURMOUNT-4 trial showed up to 26% loss in some cohorts. However, head-to-head trials are limited, and individual responses vary significantly.

When does weight loss plateau on GLP-1 medications?

Most patients see the fastest weight loss during months 2-6 after reaching the maintenance dose. Weight loss continues but slows through months 6-12, and typically plateaus around months 15-18. Some patients continue to lose small amounts beyond 18 months, especially with diet and exercise optimization.

What happens if I stop taking a GLP-1 medication?

The STEP 1 extension trial showed that patients regained about two-thirds of their lost weight within one year of stopping semaglutide. This is a key consideration — most experts recommend planning for long-term use. Lifestyle changes made during treatment can help reduce (but not eliminate) rebound.

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator uses population averages from published clinical trials. About 33% of patients lose more than the average, 33% hit roughly the average, and 33% lose less. Factors including genetics, diet, exercise, starting BMI, dose achieved, and medication adherence all significantly affect individual outcomes.

What are common side effects of GLP-1 medications?

The most common side effects are gastrointestinal — nausea (30-45% of patients), vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation. These are usually worst during dose escalation and improve over time. Serious but rare risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, and thyroid C-cell tumors (seen in animal studies). Always discuss risks with your doctor.
