How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

The relationship between horsepower, torque, and RPM is defined by a single fundamental formula used throughout automotive and mechanical engineering.

The basic rule:

  • The formula: HP = Torque (lb-ft) × RPM ÷ 5,252
  • Rearranged for torque: Torque = HP × 5,252 ÷ RPM
  • Rearranged for RPM: RPM = HP × 5,252 ÷ Torque

The constant 5,252 comes from unit conversions: 1 HP = 33,000 ft-lbs/min, and since torque is measured in lb-ft while power is in ft-lbs/min, dividing 33,000 by 2π gives 5,252. This is also why horsepower and torque curves always cross at exactly 5,252 RPM on a dyno chart.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do horsepower and torque always cross at 5,252 RPM?

At 5,252 RPM, the formula HP = Torque × RPM / 5,252 simplifies to HP = Torque. This mathematical relationship means the two values are always equal at exactly 5,252 RPM, regardless of the engine. It's a consequence of the unit definitions, not engine design.

What is more important, horsepower or torque?

Torque determines how hard the engine can push (acceleration feel and towing ability), while horsepower determines the top speed and overall rate of work. For towing and off-the-line acceleration, torque matters more. For top speed and high-RPM performance, horsepower matters more.

How do I measure my car's horsepower?

A chassis dynamometer (dyno) measures wheel horsepower by spinning the drive wheels against a drum. This gives you actual power at the wheels after drivetrain losses. Crank horsepower (advertised by manufacturers) is typically 15-20% higher than wheel horsepower.

What is the difference between crank HP and wheel HP?

Crank HP is measured at the engine's crankshaft and is the number manufacturers advertise. Wheel HP is measured at the wheels after drivetrain losses (transmission, differential, axles). Typical drivetrain loss is 15% for manual and 18-20% for automatic transmissions.

Can I convert metric kW to horsepower?

Yes. 1 horsepower (mechanical) = 0.7457 kilowatts. To convert kW to HP, divide by 0.7457 (or multiply by 1.341). For example, 100 kW equals about 134 HP. European cars often list power in kW or PS (Pferdestärke), where 1 PS = 0.9863 HP.

Why does a diesel engine have high torque but lower horsepower?

Diesel engines produce high torque at low RPM due to their high compression ratios and long stroke designs. But since HP = Torque × RPM / 5252, the low RPM range means horsepower numbers stay moderate. A diesel making 400 lb-ft at 2,000 RPM only produces about 152 HP.
