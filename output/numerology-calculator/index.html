How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

Numerology assigns numeric values to your name and birthdate, then reduces them to single digits (or master numbers 11, 22, 33) to reveal personality traits and life themes.

The basic rule:

  • Life Path Number: Sum all digits of your birthdate, reduce to single digit or master number
  • Expression Number: Assign A=1 through Z=26 to each letter in your full name, sum and reduce
  • Soul Urge Number: Same process but using only vowels (A, E, I, O, U) from your name

Master numbers 11, 22, and 33 are not reduced further as they carry special significance in numerology. Your Life Path is considered the most important number in your numerology chart.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Life Path number?

The Life Path number is derived from your full date of birth and is considered the most important number in numerology. It reveals your life purpose, natural talents, and the opportunities and challenges you may encounter.

What are master numbers in numerology?

Master numbers are 11, 22, and 33. They are not reduced to single digits because they carry intensified energy and special significance. Master number 11 relates to intuition, 22 to master building, and 33 to master teaching.

Should I use my birth name or current name?

Traditional numerology uses your full birth name as it appears on your birth certificate. This is believed to carry the vibration you were meant to have. However, some practitioners also analyze current names for insight into present energies.

What is the Expression number?

The Expression number (also called Destiny number) is calculated from all the letters in your full birth name. It reveals your natural abilities, personal goals, and the talents you have at your disposal throughout life.

What is the Soul Urge number?

The Soul Urge number (also called Heart's Desire) is calculated using only the vowels in your full birth name. It reveals your inner motivations, deepest desires, and what truly drives you at a soul level.

How do you reduce numbers in numerology?

To reduce a number, add all its digits together. If the result is more than one digit (and not a master number 11, 22, or 33), add the digits again. Repeat until you get a single digit. For example: 29 becomes 2+9=11 (master number, stop).
