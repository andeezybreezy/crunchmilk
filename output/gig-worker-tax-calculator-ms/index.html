How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

This calculator estimates the total tax burden for 1099/self-employed gig workers, including self-employment tax and federal income tax, then calculates quarterly estimated payments and the equivalent W-2 salary.

The basic rule:

  • Self-employment tax = 92.35% of net earnings × 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
  • SE tax deduction = 50% of SE tax is deductible from gross income for income tax purposes
  • Federal income tax calculated using 2024 tax brackets after standard deduction
  • W-2 equivalent accounts for employer-paid FICA (7.65%) and typical benefits value

Gig workers effectively pay double FICA taxes since they cover both the employer and employee portions. The half-of-SE-tax deduction and business expense deductions help offset this. Set aside 25-30% of net income for taxes and pay quarterly to avoid penalties.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Mississippi handle this differently?

Mississippi has its own specific rules, rates, and limits that may differ significantly from federal guidelines or other states. This calculator uses Mississippi-specific data where available. Always verify with a local professional for important decisions.

What is self-employment tax?

Self-employment tax is 15.3% of your net earnings — 12.4% for Social Security (on income up to $168,600 in 2024) and 2.9% for Medicare (no cap). W-2 employees split this with their employer (7.65% each), but self-employed workers pay both halves.

When are quarterly estimated tax payments due?

Quarterly payments are due April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15 of the following year. Missing these deadlines can result in underpayment penalties of about 8% annually on the amount owed, even if you pay in full when filing.

What can gig workers deduct as business expenses?

Common deductions include vehicle mileage ($0.67/mile in 2024), home office, phone/internet, equipment, software/apps, health insurance premiums, retirement contributions (SEP IRA, Solo 401k), professional development, and supplies. Keep receipts and track expenses diligently.

What is the W-2 equivalent salary?

Your W-2 equivalent is the salary a traditional job would need to pay for you to take home the same amount. It accounts for the employer paying 7.65% FICA and typically contributing to health insurance (~$6,000/yr) and retirement (~3% match). A $75,000 1099 income often equals about a $60,000-$65,000 W-2 salary.

How much should gig workers set aside for taxes?

A safe rule is 25-30% of net income (after expenses). For higher earners (over $90,000 net), set aside 30-35%. Open a separate savings account and transfer your tax percentage with each payment you receive to avoid a painful surprise at tax time.

Can gig workers reduce their tax bill with retirement accounts?

Yes. A SEP IRA allows contributions up to 25% of net self-employment income (max $69,000 in 2024). A Solo 401(k) allows up to $23,000 employee contribution plus 25% employer contribution. These reduce both income tax and are powerful wealth-building tools.
