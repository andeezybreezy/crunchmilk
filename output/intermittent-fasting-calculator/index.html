How It Works

Intermittent fasting restricts when you eat, not what you eat. This calculator builds a personalized schedule with meal timing based on your chosen protocol.

The basic rule:

  • 16:8 is the most popular — fast 16 hours, eat within an 8-hour window
  • During the fasting window, only water, black coffee, and plain tea are allowed
  • Calorie targets are applied to the eating window — concentrate nutrition into fewer meals

Research suggests intermittent fasting can improve insulin sensitivity, support fat loss, and simplify meal planning. Start with 16:8 before trying more aggressive protocols.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which intermittent fasting protocol is best for beginners?

16:8 is the best starting protocol. Most people already fast 10-12 hours overnight, so extending to 16 hours means just skipping breakfast or having an early dinner. Once comfortable, you can try 18:6 or 20:4 if desired.

What can I consume during the fasting window?

Water, black coffee, plain tea, and sparkling water are fine during fasting. Anything with calories, sweeteners (even artificial), or cream breaks the fast. Some purists also avoid flavored teas, but black/green/herbal tea is generally accepted.

Will intermittent fasting cause muscle loss?

Not if you eat enough protein (0.7-1g per pound of body weight) and maintain a strength training routine. Research shows IF preserves muscle mass similarly to traditional dieting when protein intake is adequate.

How long does it take to see results?

Most people notice reduced bloating and improved energy within the first week. Visible fat loss typically appears after 2-4 weeks of consistent fasting combined with a slight calorie deficit. Patience and consistency are key.

Is intermittent fasting safe for everyone?

IF is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women, people with a history of eating disorders, children under 18, or those with diabetes without medical supervision. Always consult a healthcare provider if you have a medical condition.

What is the 5:2 method?

The 5:2 method involves eating normally for 5 days per week and restricting calories to 500-600 on the other 2 non-consecutive days. It's less about timing and more about weekly calorie reduction. Many people find it easier than daily fasting.
