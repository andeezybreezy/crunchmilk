How the Beehive Yield Calculator Works

This calculator estimates honey production based on hive type, regional climate, and beekeeper experience.

Yield = Base Rate × Region Factor × Experience Factor × Hives
  • Langstroth: Base 50 lbs/hive, most productive design
  • Top Bar: Base 25 lbs/hive, more wax per honey lb
  • Warré: Base 30 lbs/hive, natural comb building
  • Wax yield: ~1 lb wax per 6-7 lbs honey harvested
  • Revenue: Calculated at $10/lb (local farmers market average)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much honey does one hive produce?

A Langstroth hive typically produces 30-60 lbs of surplus honey per year in temperate climates. Experienced beekeepers can harvest 60-100+ lbs. First-year hives usually produce little surplus.

How many jars of honey per hive?

At 50 lbs of honey per hive, you get about 40 twelve-ounce jars or 50 eight-ounce jars. One pound of honey fills roughly one 16-oz jar. Honey is denser than water at about 12 oz per cup.

How much is honey worth per pound?

Local raw honey sells for $8-$15 per pound at farmers markets and $15-$25 for specialty varieties. Wholesale to stores runs $5-$8 per pound. Value-added products like creamed honey fetch higher prices.

How much beeswax does a hive produce?

Roughly 1 lb of wax per 6-7 lbs of honey from capping wax. A hive producing 50 lbs of honey yields 2-3 lbs of harvestable wax. Top bar hives produce more wax relative to honey than Langstroth hives.

What hive type produces the most honey?

Langstroth hives are the most productive at 30-60 lbs per year. Top bar hives average 15-35 lbs and Warre hives 20-40 lbs. Langstroth's standardized frames make management and harvesting most efficient.

Do first-year hives produce honey?

First-year hives rarely produce surplus honey. The colony spends its energy building comb, raising brood, and storing enough for winter. Most beekeepers wait until the second year to harvest.
