How It Works

Notary fees are regulated by state law, with each state setting maximum allowable charges per notarial act.

The basic rule:

  • Most states cap fees at $2–$15 per notarization for in-person services
  • Remote Online Notarization (RON) typically costs $25 per notarization and is legal in most states
  • Mobile notaries charge travel fees of $25–$100+ on top of per-signature fees
  • Real estate closings often involve multiple notarizations and may have higher total costs

Some banks and credit unions offer free notary services to account holders. UPS Store, FedEx Office, and AAA offices also provide notary services at or near state maximum rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a notary cost?

Notary fees range from $2 to $15 per notarization in most states for in-person service. Remote Online Notarization (RON) typically costs $25. Mobile notaries who travel to you charge additional travel fees of $25-$100 or more depending on distance.

What is Remote Online Notarization (RON)?

RON allows documents to be notarized via a secure video call. The signer and notary connect through an approved platform with identity verification. RON is legal in most states and typically costs $25 per notarization. It's convenient for people who can't visit a notary in person.

Can I get free notary services?

Yes. Many banks and credit unions provide free notary services to their account holders. Some libraries, government offices, and employers also offer free notarization. Call ahead to confirm availability and any document limitations.

How much does a mobile notary cost?

A mobile notary charges the state-regulated fee per notarization plus a travel fee, typically $25-$100 depending on distance and time. For after-hours, weekend, or rush requests, expect to pay $75-$150+ in travel fees.

Do notary fees vary by document type?

The per-notarization fee is the same regardless of document type in most states. However, real estate documents often require more notarizations (sometimes 5-15 signatures), increasing the total cost. Some states have specific fee schedules for certain document types.

What do I need to bring to a notary?

You need the unsigned document (never sign before the notary), a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, or state ID), and any required witnesses. The notary will verify your identity and watch you sign. Some documents may require additional forms of ID.
