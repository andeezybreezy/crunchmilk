How the Candle Wax Calculator Works

This calculator determines how much wax, fragrance oil, and dye you need based on your container size and wax type.

Wax Weight = Container Volume × Fill % × Wax Density
  • Fill percentage (typically 85%) leaves room for the wick and prevents overflow
  • Wax density varies by type — soy is lighter than paraffin or beeswax
  • Fragrance oil is calculated as a percentage of the wax weight (6-10% typical)
  • Dye amount is estimated at ~0.1 oz per pound of wax for medium color

Example: An 8 oz container with soy wax at 85% fill = 8 × 0.85 × 0.86 = 5.85 oz of wax per candle.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much wax do I need for a candle?

The amount depends on your container volume and wax type. Multiply the container volume by the fill percentage (85%) and the wax density. For soy wax, an 8 oz container needs about 5.8 oz of wax.

What is fragrance load percentage?

Fragrance load is the percentage of fragrance oil relative to wax weight. Most waxes support 6-10%. Going above the recommended maximum can cause sweating, poor burn, or safety issues.

What is the density of soy wax?

Soy wax has a density of approximately 0.86 oz per fluid ounce (0.90 g/ml). It is lighter than paraffin (0.90 oz/fl oz) and beeswax (0.96 oz/fl oz).

How much dye do I use per pound of wax?

For liquid dye, use 3-10 drops per pound. For dye blocks, approximately 0.1-0.2 oz per pound. Start light and increase for deeper colors.

Should I fill my candle container all the way?

No. Fill to 85-90% capacity to leave room for the wick, prevent overflow when the wax melts, and allow for a clean finish. This calculator accounts for fill percentage.

What's the difference between soy and paraffin wax?

Soy wax is plant-based, burns cleaner with less soot, and has a lower melting point. Paraffin is petroleum-based, provides better scent throw, and produces more vibrant colors. Coconut blends offer a middle ground.
