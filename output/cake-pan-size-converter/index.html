How to Convert Oven Recipes to Air Fryer

Converting between cake pan sizes is all about matching the batter volume. The key is calculating the area of each pan and using the ratio to scale ingredients.

The basic rule:

  • Calculate the area of each pan: round = π × r², square = side², rectangle = width × length
  • Find the scale factor: target area ÷ original area
  • Multiply all ingredient amounts by the scale factor. Adjust baking time: larger pans may need slightly lower temp and longer time; smaller pans the opposite.

When scaling up, consider using multiple pans of the original size instead. When scaling down significantly, reduce oven temperature by 25°F and check for doneness earlier. Batter depth affects baking time more than total volume.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I substitute a square pan for a round pan?

Yes. A square pan with sides about 2 inches shorter than the round pan's diameter has roughly the same area. For example, an 8-inch round pan is close in area to a 7-inch square pan. Use this calculator for exact conversions.

How do I adjust baking time for a different pan size?

Thinner layers bake faster, thicker layers take longer. When using a larger pan (thinner batter), reduce time by 5-10 minutes and check early. For smaller pans (thicker batter), add 5-15 minutes. Always test with a toothpick for doneness.

What if I only have a 9×13 rectangle and the recipe calls for a round pan?

A 9×13 inch rectangle (117 sq in) is about 2.3 times the area of an 8-inch round (50.3 sq in). You would need to roughly double the recipe. This calculator gives you the exact scale factor.

Does pan material affect the conversion?

Pan material affects baking time and temperature, not ingredient amounts. Dark metal pans absorb more heat—reduce temp by 25°F. Glass pans also conduct heat differently—reduce temp by 25°F. Silicone pans may need slightly longer baking times.

Can I use two smaller pans instead of one large one?

Absolutely. Add the areas of the two smaller pans together and compare to your original. Two 8-inch round pans (100.5 sq in total) are close to one 9×13 rectangle (117 sq in). Bake at the same temperature but check for doneness earlier since layers are thinner.

What about springform pans and bundt pans?

Springform pans are measured the same as round pans. Bundt pans are trickier because they have a center hole and are measured by volume (cups). A standard 10-inch bundt holds about 12 cups of batter, roughly equal to a 9×13 rectangle.
