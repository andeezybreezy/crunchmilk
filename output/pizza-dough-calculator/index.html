How It Works

Baker's percentages express every ingredient as a percentage of total flour weight. This system makes it easy to scale recipes while keeping ratios consistent.

The basic rule:

  • Flour = 100% (the base — all other ingredients are relative to flour)
  • Water = hydration % (63% means 630g water per 1000g flour)
  • Salt = 2.5–3%, Yeast = 0.1–0.3%, Oil = 0–3%

Ball weight is calculated from pizza size. Lower yeast percentages require longer fermentation but develop more complex flavor. A 24-hour cold ferment with 0.1-0.2% yeast is standard for Neapolitan pizza.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is baker's percentage?

Baker's percentage expresses each ingredient as a percentage of the total flour weight. Flour is always 100%. If you have 1000g flour and 63% hydration, you use 630g water. This makes scaling easy — just change the flour amount and all other ingredients adjust proportionally.

What hydration should I use?

Neapolitan style uses 60-65% hydration for a supple, slightly chewy dough. New York style uses 65-70% for a foldable, slightly puffier crust. Higher hydration (70%+) creates lighter, more airy crusts but is harder to handle. Start at 63% and adjust from there.

How much yeast should I use?

It depends on fermentation time. For a 24-hour cold ferment, use 0.1-0.2% instant yeast. For same-day dough (4-6 hours at room temperature), use 0.5-1%. For a quick 1-2 hour rise, use 1-1.5%. Less yeast with longer fermentation gives better flavor.

What flour should I use?

For Neapolitan, use Tipo 00 flour (like Caputo Pizzeria). For NY style, bread flour or a blend of bread flour and all-purpose works great. The higher protein in bread flour gives a chewier texture. All-purpose works in a pinch but gives a softer crust.

How long should I ferment the dough?

A 24-hour cold ferment in the refrigerator gives the best flavor. You can also do a 48-72 hour cold ferment for even more complex flavors. Same-day dough (2-4 hours at room temperature) works but won't develop as much flavor. Pull dough from fridge 1-2 hours before shaping.

Should I add olive oil to pizza dough?

Traditional Neapolitan dough uses no oil. NY style and pan pizza recipes often include 2-3% olive oil for a slightly softer crumb and richer flavor. Oil also makes the dough easier to stretch. Skip it for authentic Neapolitan; add it for everything else.
